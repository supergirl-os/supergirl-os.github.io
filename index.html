    Recent Research

    Here are some of the recent studies I have conducted.

  • Weakly supervised learning combined with temporal anomaly detection

    • We introduce a weakly supervised approach to the field of temporal anomaly detection in order to obtain better performance using fewer labels. This study is currently underway.

  • Research on Urban Safety Perception

    • With the acceleration of urbanization, the issue of urban livability is receiving more and more attention. As a gathering place for urban residents to live, the street scene profoundly affects the residents' experience and physical and mental health in urban activities. We first built a crowdsourcing website to obtain the public's perception of the safety of street view images to form a dataset.

    We concentrate on two directions:
      1.Leverage reinforcement learning-based decision framework to obtain human-like safety perceptions.
      2.Use inverse reinforcement learning (IRL) to recover the reward function that can explain the evaluation pattern and help experts quantitatively analyze perceptual features.

    I currently have two papers under review (one as first author, the other as a co-author)

  • Autonomous Robot Shooting and Movement in Specific Maps

    • Based on visual perception and Cost map, we used behaviour tree to realize intelligent decision-making of the automatic robot. So the robot can move and shoot on a specific map.

    I am mainly responsible for the construction of the robot decision-making framework. Also, as the project leader, I need to control the research progress and the funding application.
    Our results won the ICRA RMUA International Third Prize.
